Silent Disaster
Flood Risk · Early Signal System · India v1.0

Risk awareness
before a disaster
becomes one.

A transparent, rule-based environmental monitoring system that surfaces early flood risk signals for Indian regions — before damage makes the situation visible.

Why this matters

Most flood-related decisions happen after conditions are already critical. Drainage systems overflow, evacuation routes flood, and emergency resources are deployed reactively. This system is designed to shift that window earlier — not to predict, but to flag when environmental signals are trending toward risk.

What this is not

This is not a machine learning system. There is no black box. Every score is generated from transparent, auditable rules based on rainfall trends, historical flood sensitivity, and temperature interactions. The logic is fully readable.

What this covers

Eight Indian cities with distinct flood risk profiles — from the Brahmaputra basin in Assam to the semi-arid plateau of Rajasthan. Each region is assessed on 7-day or 14-day environmental windows.

Environmental data: simulated from historical patterns Risk logic: transparent, rule-based · No ML involved
